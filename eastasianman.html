Evidence




Woman: "He's from Brooklyn."

Woman's mom: "But he's Chinese, right?" "Is there a Bloomingdale in China?""How tall is he?"

Womam: "Tall enough."

Explanation


The female photographer's mom keeps asking her about her new boyfriend because he is Chinese. She lacks enough knowledge about China and is a bit prejudiced and influenced by stereotypes. The eye-covering gesture symbolizes the fact appearance and race matters little to a relationship. The mother is unjustified for saying "tall enough" because, though it might be true that the physique of Asians is *generally* smaller than the Caucasian race, love is an emotion. It is not about picking the tallest or most handsome guy.
