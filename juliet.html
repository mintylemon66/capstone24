Romeo and Juliet | Juliet



Evidence

Yea, noise? Then I’ll be brief. O happy dagger! This is thy sheath; there rust, and let me die. (5.3.169-170)


Explanation

It is interesting how poison is considered a feminine way to kill oneself while knifing oneself is considered more masculine, but Juliet stabbed herself and Romeo poisoned himself. The end of the play also states “Juliet and her Romeo”, and throughout the play Romeo seems to be the more immature or feminine figure. I wonder if Shakespeare is trying to enhance female rights and show that females can be stronger than males. “This is thy sheath” shows that Juliet is very brave, and this metaphor is sad but realistic.
