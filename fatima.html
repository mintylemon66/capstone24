Evidence

“The tribesmen are always in search of treasure...and women of the desert are proud of their tribesmen.” (Coelho 89)

“I’m a desert woman, and I’m proud of that. I want my husband to wander as free as the wind that shapes the dunes. And, if I have to, I will accept the fact that he has become a part of the clouds, and the animals, and the water of the desert.” (Coelho 91)


Explanation

The second of the four obstacles is the fear of disappointing loved ones. The support from Fatima for Santiago defies this fear because those who truly love one will want one to achieve one’s personal legend regardless of one being with them or not. the author’s writing here made me feel uncomfortable because the women were portrayed as meek and dependent. The structure of the society in this book is patriarchal, where women are all one-dimensionally supportive of men. I wonder if the gender roles have reversed will people still think this way? When women want to pursue their dreams and travel far from their homes, will men stay, maintain the house and wait for the women loyally?
